The Ontario Primary Care Action Plan

FHC received provincial funding to attach approximately 6,000 new clients to primary care in East & North Toronto. This isn't an abstract goal — it's a person waiting for a doctor. Active outreach means referrals are accelerating daily.

🌉
Your role as the bridge

From first contact to attachment, the intake team connects patients to the care they need. Every step you take — every call made, every tracker updated — moves someone closer to having a family doctor.

The Five Key Messages for New Patients

1
Yes, FHC IS open for new primary care clients

2
First step: Complete the Oceans Outreach Form

Online, in-person, phone, or email. Staff can help. One form per person, same form for all FHC sites.

3
Someone will contact them within 10 days

This is our standard. Track it and meet it.

4

5

Flemingdon, Fairview, HATP, MGH, NYGH — one form does it all.

Intake Workflow — Step by Step
The four key steps every patient goes through (note: varies slightly by site)
🗒️
Site Variation Note

The workflow below is the core process. Individual sites (Flemingdon, Fairview, HATP, MGH, NYGH) may have slight variations. When in doubt, check with your intake lead.

1
OCEAN Form Completed

Client completes the online Oceans Outreach Form. Staff can assist in person, by phone, or by email. One form per person — works across all FHC sites.

Who: Patient / CHA / HINC

2
Mini Chart Created in PSS

Secretary creates the chart. Purple Tracker is added immediately. Forms are sent to client via OCEAN secure email. Chart is backdated to the date of creation.

Who: Medical Secretary

3
Assessment & Triage

HINC calls the patient. A triage code is assigned (Red / Yellow / Green). The HEQ form is completed. Equity flags are documented — language, immigration status, safety, complexity.

Who: HINC / Intake Worker

4
Booking or Transfer

Based on triage code and postal code: book Access Clinic appointment, Meet & Greet, or transfer to an external partner organization.

Who: Medical Secretary / HINC

Roles & Responsibilities
Who does what — think of it as the front-of-house vs. back-of-house in a restaurant
🍽️
The Restaurant Analogy

The Medical Secretary is like the host — first contact, seating (chart creation), managing bookings. The HINC is the server — the real relationship builder who takes the full order, understands complex needs, and navigates the kitchen (system) for the patient.

Medical Secretary / CHA

  • Create mini chart for every new client
  • Add and complete the Purple Tracker
  • Send forms via OCEAN (Intake Package #1)
  • Schedule appointments once forms are verified
  • Prioritize urgent and navigation-free cases
  • Monitor and update patient status in EMR

HINC / Intake Worker

  • Call patients — confirm details & needs
  • Explain patient-provider relationship & expectations
  • Assign triage code (Red / Yellow / Green)
  • Complete HEQ form
  • Arrange interpreters when needed
  • Document encounter & message Medical Secretary
💬
Communication Channels

Patient communication: Always through PSS messages (secure, documented, traceable).
Team communication: Microsoft Teams for general coordination.

→ Book a Meet & Greet appointment

May direct to FV MDAC, PCNC, HATP, FL, or NYGH Access Clinics

🟢 GREEN

Who: Patients who just need a family doctor — no urgent or complex needs

→ Give contact number; patient calls if issues arise

📏
Appointment Lengths

RED appointments: 30 minutes in English, 45 minutes when an interpreter is needed. Always confirm interpreter arrangements before booking.

Partner Transfer Lane

Some patients — based on postal code, language, or capacity — may be routed to external partner organizations rather than booked internally. This is a valid triage outcome and must be documented using the Cat 3 External Referral statuses.

The Purple Tracker
Your most important documentation tool — the sticky note on every patient file that the whole team can see
📌
The Sticky Note Analogy

The Purple Tracker is like a visible sticky note on the front of a patient's physical file — except everyone on the team can see it in real time, it's timestamped, and it never falls off. Every time a patient moves, the note gets updated.

What the Purple Tracker IS

📄 A Form in PSS

Full name: "FHC – Consultation Request – FHC TNO Intake – New". Added to every new patient chart.

📊 A Real-Time Dashboard

Shows every patient's current status at a glance — like a live scoreboard for intake.

🕐 A Timestamp Log

Every status update is date-stamped. Creates a full audit trail from Day 0 to rostering.

💬 A Team Communication Tool

When a HINC updates the tracker, Medical Secretaries and managers see it immediately.

How to Add the Purple Tracker (6 Steps)

1
Open the patient chart in PSS

Press Ctrl + ] to open the chart quickly.

2
Insert the tracker form

Add: "FHC – Consultation Request – FHC TNO Intake – New". Back-date it to the date the chart was created.

3
Fill ALL mandatory fields

Urgency lane · Source (HCC list / Ocean form / In person / Phone) · Postal Code · Referral Status · OHT region

4
Set the opening status

"Chart Created: Pre-Attachment" is always the first status. This is Stage 0.

5
Update status at every milestone

Call made → forms sent → appointment booked → each time, update the status immediately. Not tomorrow — now.

6
Add the PSS Dashboard

Main PSS window → Dashboard → Add (+) → "FHC – Consultation Request – FHC TNO Intake – New". Shows all active patients at a glance.

Why Updating It Matters

📈
Your workload becomes visible

An accurate tracker shows how many patients each role is managing — essential for fair resource planning.

🔔
7-day alert rule

Stale statuses unchanged for 7+ days trigger automated alerts. Updating keeps patients moving.

📌
The Golden Rule

Update the tracker every single time a patient's status changes. Not tomorrow — now. Monthly reporting is NOT tracked by this tool — it tracks individual patient journeys.

Health Care Connect (HCC) & Call Scripts
The HCC workflow and what to say on every call

HCC Workflow — 5 Steps

1
HCC List Received

Add list clients to EMR immediately. The HCC list must NOT be stored outside the EMR and must be discarded after use.

2
Mini Charts Created

Medical Secretary OR HINCs create mini charts for all new clients on the list.

3
Purple Tracker Inserted

Added to every chart immediately upon list processing.

4
HINCs Call Patients

Track status in PSS after each call. All data in EMR only — no external spreadsheets.

5
Data Reported Regularly

Referrals received, Transferred-pending, and Transferred-confirmed are reported to funders.

Sample Call Script

OPENER: "Hi, I'm [Your Name] from Flemingdon Health Centre. Your name came from the Health Care Connect list." Q1: Do you already have a family doctor? → YES: Note the doctor's name → document in tracker → NO: "We can help — let's start with the HEQ form" Q2: Do you need language support? → YES: Arrange interpreter now, or call back with interpreter → NO: Send secure email (password = date of birth) Q3: Do you need an appointment soon? → YES (urgent): Book in appropriate Access Clinic → NO: Send secure email (password = email address)

After the Call — Post-Call Actions

1
Message the Medical Secretary

Include: patient name, outcome of call (has doctor / needs attachment), urgent needs, interpreter requirements, confirmation that forms were sent.

2
Document the Encounter in PSS

Use the EMR shortcut toolbar. Record: date/time, patient response, language needs, and next steps. Use the 'HCC Intake' or 'CHW Call' template.

🔒
Key Data Rules

All data recorded in EMR (PSS) only — no external spreadsheets. HCC lists must be discarded after use. Patient communication via PSS messages. Team communication via Teams.

Supplemental Options

IFH (Interim Federal Health)
Covered under Blue Cross. If rejected, client becomes uninsured. During appeal = uninsured status.

Aboriginal Non-Insured Health Benefits
Supplemental coverage — document in chart accordingly.

Third Party Private Insurer
Super visa holders, students, visitors — not uninsured, but should have private insurance.

Where to Document Insurance

📍
Documentation Locations

Record insurance status in the comment section of demographics (visible on chart's left corner). IFH details in the Insurance Number field AND comments. File a copy of IFH document with UCI in chart. Update in HEQ form in PSS.

Frequently Asked Questions
Common questions patients ask — and how to answer them

Q: Can patients request a female provider?

A: Discouraged — we do our best to honour provider preferences, but availability depends on capacity. Document the request during intake.

Q: Can family members see the same doctor?

A: We try to keep families together, but if a panel is full we assign another team member. All providers work as coordinated teams.

Q: Is switching providers possible?

A: Discouraged — depends on capacity and continuity of care. Requests are reviewed carefully by intake.

Q: What if a patient is on a work permit or visit visa?

A: If they have OHIP, we take them as patients. If they may not have OHIP, refer to the appropriate organization.

Q: What does a patient need for their first appointment?

A: Health card (if available), insurance documents, medication list, pharmacy info, and any medical records. Arrive on time; 24hr notice to cancel.

Q: Which partner organizations might patients be transferred to?

A: HATM, Albany Medical, Magenta Health, Don Mills FHT, Bridgepoint FHT, SETFHT, Unify Health, Access Alliance, Generations, South Riverdale CHC, Avesta Medical — based on postal code and capacity.
